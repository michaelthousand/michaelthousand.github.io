About Michael

I grew up surrounded by technology. My parents owned their own software sales company when I was growing up and I took apart my first computer at 7 years old. I have been modding video games in my spare time for years and am now hoping to launch a new career working with one of my first loves: tech.

I began my undergraduate career as a psychology student before ultimately turning to philosophy. I noticed quickly that philosophical logic proofs had a number of similarities to computer programming. This allowed me to quickly teach myself how to read lines of code and change the behavior of the video games I was playing. I began customizing all of the games I was playing to be exactly how I wanted them, complete with custom characters, events, and more.

For the past three years, I have been working for a healthcare company in Wisconsin. I recently began a certificate program at the University of California - Berkeley for Software Development and Programming. I also have been working through CodeAcademy's program for Full-Stack Engineering. I am hoping to use this website to display my skills as I develop them. You will find a table below with where I feel I am at in each technical category (as well as some other skills I have acquired through my current and previous careers). You will also find a brief introduction to my work and education background.

I am hoping to continue developing at a rapid pace and move into a position where I can work on application/video game development or in cybersecurity. If you believe that I may be a fit for your company or project, please feel free to reach out via the Contact page.
